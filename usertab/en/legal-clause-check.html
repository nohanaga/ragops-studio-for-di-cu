# Severity Clause Issue Details
1 Critical Article 5 (Contract Term)
The auto-renewal clause lacks a notice deadline for renewal refusal. This raises concerns about consistency with mandatory provisions such as the Land and Building Lease Act.
"The effective period of this agreement shall be one year from the date of execution, and shall be automatically renewed upon expiration."
→ A renewal refusal notice deadline (e.g., 30 days before expiration) must be added
2 Critical Article 12 (Damages)
No cap on damages is specified, creating a risk of unlimited liability.
"Party B shall compensate all damages arising from a breach of this agreement by Party A."
→ A limitation clause such as "up to the total consideration paid by Party A to Party B under this agreement" should be added
3 Critical — (No clause)
No governing law or jurisdiction provision exists anywhere in the contract. This creates a risk of jurisdictional disputes in case of conflict.
→ A clause such as "This agreement shall be governed by the laws of Japan, and the Tokyo District Court shall have exclusive jurisdiction as the court of first instance" should be added
4 Warning Article 8 (Termination)
Termination grounds are listed exhaustively, but a general breach of trust clause is missing.
"Party A may terminate this agreement without prior notice if Party B falls under any of the following."
→ Adding a general clause such as "any other action that significantly damages the relationship of trust" is recommended
5 Warning Article 15 (Force Majeure)
The definition of force majeure does not include epidemics/pandemics.
→ Adding "epidemics, spread of infectious diseases" to force majeure events is recommended
6 Warning Article 3 (Outsourced Services)
The subcontracting provision only requires prior written consent, leaving management responsibility for subcontractors unclear.
→ Adding a joint liability clause requiring subcontractors to bear equivalent obligations is recommended
7 Warning Article 10 (Confidentiality)
The survival period of confidentiality obligations states only "after termination of this agreement" without specifying a concrete timeframe.
→ A specific survival period such as "3 years after termination" should be specified
📌 Recommended Actions (by Priority)
  • 1. Immediate (Critical): Set a damages cap and add a governing law/jurisdiction clause. These are directly linked to the contract's validity and enforceability.
  • 2. Urgent: Add a renewal refusal notice deadline to the auto-renewal clause. If applicable to consumer contracts, verify consistency with the Consumer Contract Act.
  • 3. Next Revision: Expand termination grounds, update force majeure definition, clarify subcontractor management responsibility, and specify the confidentiality survival period.
